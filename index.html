Voting for the right anwser: Adversarial defense for speaker verification

Authors: Haibin Wu*, Yang Zhang*, Zhiyong Wu, Dong Wang, Hung-yi Lee

Abstract: Automatic speaker verification (ASV) is a mature technology for biometric identification, and has been ubiquitous implemented in security-critic applications, such as banking and access control. However, previous works have shown that ASV is under the radar of adversarial attacks, which are over 99% similar to their genuine counterparts from human's perception, yet will manipulate the ASV render wrong prediction. Due to the very late emergence of adversarial attacks for ASV, effective countermeasures against them are rare and limited. Given that the security of ASV is of high priority, in this work, we propose to harness the idea of "voting for the right answer" to conduct data augmentation during inference time and equip the ASV with the capacity of countering adversarial attacks. The experimental results show that our proposed method accomplishes effective defense performance against both the limited-knowledge attacks by pulling the adversarial samples out of the blind spots, and the perfect-knowledge attackers by introducing randomness and causing the attack gradient unstable and obfuscated.
